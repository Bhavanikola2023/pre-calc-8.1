Law of Sines Formula
What is the Law of Sines formula?
(Click to reveal the formula)
a / sin(A) = b / sin(B) = c / sin(C)
This formula relates the sides of a triangle to the sines of the angles opposite those sides.

Study Tips

Tips for Mastering the Law of Sines

  • Recognize Triangle Cases: The Law of Sines is useful for SAA, ASA, and SSA cases.
  • Check for No Solution: If sin(A) > 1 when solving for angle A, no triangle exists.
  • Ambiguous Case: SSA can result in 0, 1, or 2 triangles depending on measurements.
  • Find Missing Angles: Remember that A + B + C = 180° in any triangle.
  • Calculator Usage: Use inverse sine (sin⁻¹) for accurate angle calculations.
